Our Journey

From Struggling Parents
to Creating What We Couldn't Find

This is the story of how two parents, raising their children on Quran and Sunnah, decided to create the Islamic education resources they wished existed.

The Struggle Every Muslim Parent Knows

When our first child turned 3, we faced something we didn't expect.

We wanted to raise our children on Quran and Sunnah. We wanted them to love Allah, remember Him in everything they did, and understand His beautiful names and attributes.

But there was a problem.

Everywhere we looked, we saw screens. YouTube videos. Cartoons. Apps. Our children were drawn to them like magnets. And while some content was "Islamic," most of it was just... entertainment.

We wanted less screen time. We wanted our children attached to books.

So we searched. We looked for Islamic books that would engage young minds. Books that would teach them to reflect on Allah's creation, not just memorize facts.

What we found disappointed us.

Most books were general knowledge with an "Islamic" label slapped on. Or they mixed culture with religion. Or they were boring textbooks that even we didn't want to read.

None of them taught Tadabbur—deep reflection on Allah's words and creation.

We realized something: If we couldn't find what we needed... maybe it didn't exist yet.

The Moment Everything Changed

We were homeschooling our two children. And as any homeschooling parent knows, you quickly realize: You're not just a teacher. You're a curriculum creator.

One day, our children challenged us.

Not with words, but with their eyes. The way they looked at the books we brought them. The way they got distracted. The way they didn't connect.

That's when we understood: If we wanted something different for our children, we had to create it ourselves.

We sat down together—me (Shayan) and my wife (Umm Abdullah)—and asked ourselves:

"What do we actually want our children to learn? Not just know... but truly understand and live?"

The answer was clear: We want them to remember Allah in every moment of their day-to-day lives.

Not just during Salah. Not just when reading Quran. But when they see a tree. When they eat food. When they play. When they sleep.

We wanted them to see Allah's signs everywhere. To reflect. To connect. To love Him through understanding.

That's Tadabbur.

And that's what we decided to build.

How We Built TadabburKids

Umm Abdullah

The Book Creator

My wife, Umm Abdullah, took on the books. Not because of her Masters degree in English Literature (though that helped). But because she practices Tadabbur in her own life.

Tadabbur changed her. It made her connect to Allah in ways she never thought possible. She wanted that for our children. For all Muslim children.

But there was a challenge: How do you teach Tadabbur—something adults struggle with—to children?

She spent months figuring it out. Testing with our kids. Watching what made their eyes light up. What made them ask questions. What made them remember Allah naturally.

The answer? Make it interactive. Make it visual. Make it from their perspective.

That's how our first book was born: "Who Made All This?"—following a curious ladybug discovering Allah's creation, with flaps to lift, dhikr to recite, and questions that make children think.

Every page was designed to help children reflect, not just read.

Shayan Qureshi

The Quran Teacher

I (Shayan) took on the lessons. In 2021, I completed memorizing the Quran—a journey that changed my life. But memorization was just the beginning.

I wanted to understand what I memorized. I wanted to live it.

Teaching our own children taught me something I never learned in any class: Patience. Love. Meeting them where they are.

I realized that children aren't small adults. They learn differently. They need you to speak their language, not force them into yours.

That's when I decided to pursue my Ijazah from Masjid Nabawi in Madinah—learning Qaaidah teaching methodology directly from scholars at the Prophet's Mosque ﷺ.

Why Masjid Nabawi? Because I love it. Because learning in the presence of Rasulullah's ﷺ resting place makes everything more meaningful. Because I wanted to teach the way the Sahabah taught their children.

Now, I teach other children the way I teach my own: with patience, love, and a focus on connection—not just recitation.

Because when children understand why they're reading, they fall in love with the Quran. And that's what we want for every child.

What Makes TadabburKids Different?

We Teach Tadabbur to Children

Most people think Tadabbur is only for adults. But we believe children can learn to reflect on Allah's words and creation—when it's taught in a way they understand. That's our unique approach.

Everything is Rooted in Quran & Sunnah

We don't mix culture with religion. We don't water down Islam. Every lesson, every book, every word is based on authentic sources. We focus on Allah's Tawheed and His correct attributes—because that's the foundation of everything.

Built by Parents, For Parents

We're not corporate educators. We're parents raising our own children on these same materials. We understand your struggles because we live them every day. We know what works because we test it with our own kids first.

We Speak to Kids, Not Down to Them

The hardest part of creating TadabburKids? Making it kid-friendly. Speaking from their perspective. Understanding how they see the world. That's what makes our content different—it meets children where they are.

Our Mission Today

Every day, we wake up with one goal:

To help Muslim children remember Allah in every moment of their day-to-day lives.

Not just when they're in "Islamic class."

But when they see a bird. When they drink water. When they wake up. When they go to sleep.

We want children to see Allah's signs everywhere.

That's why we created our books. That's why we teach our lessons. That's why TadabburKids exists.

Because when children learn to reflect on Allah's creation, something beautiful happens: They fall in love with their Creator.

And that love? It stays with them forever.

Where We're Going

TadabburKids is just the beginning.

Right now, we have one book and live Quran lessons. But we dream bigger.

We want to build a full Islamic education academy—a place where Muslim families worldwide can access authentic Quranic education through books, lessons, and resources that make Tadabbur a natural part of childhood.

We don't know exactly what that will look like yet. We're taking it step by step, trusting Allah to guide us.

But we know this: We're building this as Sadaqah Jariyah.

Every child who learns to love Allah through our books... Every student who memorizes Quran with understanding through our lessons... Every family that benefits from what we create...

That's our legacy. That's what will continue long after we're gone.

And we invite you to be part of it from the beginning.

Join us in this journey. Use our books with your children. Enroll them in our lessons. Give us feedback. Share your stories.

Together, we can raise a generation that doesn't just know about Allah—they know Allah.
